Let us take you on a virtual tour through some of the most important places to visit in Dehradun





1.Sahastradhara

Sahastradhara is famous for its medicinal & therapeutic value as its water contains sulphur. As a first timer to the town, we visited the place in June'22 despite our cab driver's reluctance.

You can visit Sahastradhara all year round. Being a hill station, it experiences pleasant weather throughout the year, but if you want to see the waterfalls in full momentum, it is best to visit Dehradun from May to August.

The visitors need to pay a nominal entry fee of Rs. 250 to Rs. 300 (per person) to go on a ropeway ride. It is open every week from 9 am to 6 pm.





2.Robber's Cave

Guchu Pani or Robber's cave, whatever you may call it, this cave was a route for robbers to seek refuge during the British era. But you can take refuge in the caves for some time and enjoy the waterfalls and icy cold streams flowing in the narrow channel. It has become a popular spot for couples to visit and have a picnic inside the mammoth structure. This rock formation has a stream inside which conceals and reveals itself in a mysterious way, you will find out when you visit! We are sure this place has intrigued you already and why not! After all, it is one of the most famous Dehradun tourist places.

It is a natural cave formation where rivers flow inside the cave. The place is a popular tourist spot and which is now being maintained by Uttarakhand State. Local bus services are available up to Anarwala Village, from where it is a kilometre's trek awa





3.Rajaji National Park

Spread over a wide area, the Rajaji National Park is a dream place for all nature and animal lovers. You will get to witness a wide population of elephants, panthers, and birds here. While you enjoy the company of your new friends, you can take in the scintillating view of the Shivalik range. The park is an amalgamation of three sanctuaries- Rajaji, Motichur, and Chilla, which explains the sheer size of the estate and gives you ample opportunities to explore the wild. It is a close second to Jim Corbett park in terms of tiger reservation, which is also present in Uttarakhand! So to spend a few hours amidst mother nature and her children, be sure to visit the park and create some unforgettable memories.





4.Tapovan

Considered one of the highest grasslands in the country, Tapovan has slowly emerged as one of the best places to visit in Dehradun. The lush green surroundings all around, with the Shivling peak as its backdrop, certainly make for a beautiful sight to capture in your cameras. Legend has it that the Tapovan temple is the place where Guru Dronacharya did his penance. To all the mythology lovers, you must pay a visit here. The temple premises, as well as the valley, is the perfect place for some tranquillity and peace of mind. Many come here for some meditation and you too, can indulge. In recent times, Tapovan has become a popular trekking destination. So if you want some much-needed solitude with a side of thrilling adventure, do visit Tapovan.





5.Kalsi

This is another gem in the Dehradun district. What is so special about it? Well, its simplicity! It attracts tourists with its calm and charming environment. When you are there, do not forget to visit the historical Ashoka Rock Edict. With beautiful views of the mountains, swift streams, and narrow winding roads hugged by colourful trees, taking a tour through this village will surely be worth it.
